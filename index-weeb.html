1. What's a File Cabinet?

A file cabinet is just office furniture where you keep paper documents organized and safe. You put papers in folders and sort them by name, number, or what they're about.

2. Types of File Cabinets

A. Vertical File Cabinets

Shape: Tall and skinny.

Drawers: Usually has 2 to 5 drawers stacked up.

Good for: Small offices.

Perk: Doesn't take up much floor space.

Size: Holds letter or legal-size papers.

B. Lateral File Cabinets

Shape: Wide and short.

Drawers: Has 2 to 4 drawers next to each other.

Good for: Storing lots of files and getting to them easily.

Perk: Holds more stuff and can fit under counters.

Size: Holds letter or legal-size papers.

C. Mobile File Cabinets

Features: Small (1-3 drawers) and has wheels.

Good for: Moving around the office or keeping under your desk.

Perk: Easy to move and saves space.

D. Flat File Cabinets

Design: Big, flat drawers.

Good for: Big papers like blueprints, maps, or art.

Perk: Keeps papers flat so they don't wrinkle.

E. Fireproof File Cabinets

What they do: Keep your stuff safe from fire, smoke, and heat.

How they're made: Made with special stuff inside and tight seals.

How to know how good they are: They're rated by how many hours they can withstand fire (like 1 hour).

F. Hybrid File Cabinets

Design: Mix of vertical and lateral drawers.

What they do: Hold different sizes of papers.

3. Paper Sizes

Letter: 8.5 × 11 (regular paper size in the U.S.)

Legal: 8.5 × 14 (for longer papers)

A4: 210 × 297 mm (paper size in other countries)

4. What They're Made Of

Material | What it's like | Notes
------- | -------- | --------
Steel/Metal | Strong, can't burn easily | Common in offices
Wood | Looks nice | Heavier, burns easier
Plastic | Light, cheap | Good for home use
Aluminum | Lighter than steel, doesn't rust | Costs more

They're usually coated or painted to last longer and look good.

5. How They Lock

Key locks: Regular lock with a key.

Cam locks: You just twist to lock it.

Combination locks: Use numbers, no key.

Digital locks: Use a PIN or keypad.

Central locking: One lock locks all the drawers.

Individual locks: Each drawer locks on its own.

6. Stuff to Think About

Anti-tip thing: Only lets you open one drawer at a time so it doesn't fall over.

Smooth drawers: Drawers open quietly.

Drawers that open all the way: Easy to get to everything inside.

Label spots: So you know what's in each drawer.

Adjustable hangers: For letter or legal size papers.

Weights: Keeps it from tipping.

Wheels: So you can move it (on mobile ones).

7. How to Organize

Alphabetical: A to Z.

Numerical: By number.

Color-coded: Use colors to sort.

Chronological: By date.

Geographical: By location.

8. Staying Safe

Put heavy stuff in the bottom drawers.

Only open one drawer at a time.

Attach tall ones to the wall.

Keep the area in front clear.

Use the handles so you don't hurt your fingers.

9. How to Keep it Nice

Clean the outside with cleaner.

Put oil on the tracks so the drawers slide smoothly.

Check the locks to be sure they work.

Fix drawers that get stuck.

Don't put too much stuff in it.

10. Fire Safety

Use fire-safe ones for important papers.

Keep digital copies somewhere else.

Don't put them near heat.

11. Other Options

Digital files (scan your papers)

Keep both paper and digital copies

Storage carts you can move around

Shelves for big archives
